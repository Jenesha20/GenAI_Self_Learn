Q: What is a prescription medicine?
A: A prescription medicine is a drug that can only be provided with authorization from a licensed healthcare professional.

Q: Can I take medicine on an empty stomach?
A: Some medicines can be taken on an empty stomach, while others should be taken with food to prevent stomach irritation. Always follow label instructions.

Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?
A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is close to the next scheduled dose. Do not double the dose.

Q: Can I stop taking antibiotics once I feel better?
A: No. Always complete the full course of antibiotics to prevent resistance and recurrence of infection.

Q: Can I take two painkillers at the same time?
A: Some combinations are unsafe and may increase side effects. Always consult a pharmacist or doctor before combining pain medications.

Q: Are generic medicines safe?
A: Yes. Generic medicines contain the same active ingredient and are required to meet the same quality standards as brand-name drugs.

Q: Can pregnant women take medicines?
A: Many medicines are unsafe during pregnancy. Pregnant women should only take medicines approved by a healthcare professional.

Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking medicines?
A: Alcohol can interact with many medicines and increase side effects such as drowsiness or liver damage. Avoid alcohol unless advised otherwise.

Q: What are side effects?
A: Side effects are unwanted effects that may occur when taking a medicine. They can range from mild to serious.

Q: When should I stop taking a medicine and seek help?
A: Stop taking the medicine and seek medical attention if you experience severe allergic reactions, breathing difficulty, or severe side effects.

Q: How should medicines be stored?
A: Store medicines in a cool, dry place away from sunlight and out of reach of children unless otherwise instructed.

Q: Can I take expired medicines?
A: No. Expired medicines may be ineffective or unsafe and should be properly disposed of.

Q: Can medicines affect driving?
A: Some medicines can cause drowsiness or dizziness and may impair driving or operating machinery.

Q: Why should I not share my medicines with others?
A: Medicines are prescribed based on individual medical conditions. Sharing medicines can be dangerous.

Q: Can I crush or split tablets?
A: Some tablets should not be crushed or split, especially extended-release or coated tablets. Ask a pharmacist before doing so.

Q: What is drug interaction?
A: A drug interaction occurs when one medicine affects how another medicine works, increasing side effects or reducing effectiveness.

Q: What should I tell my doctor before starting a new medicine?
A: Inform your doctor about all medicines you take, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal products.

Q: Can herbal medicines interact with prescription drugs?
A: Yes. Herbal medicines can interact with prescription drugs and cause adverse effects.

Q: What should I do if I take too much medicine?
A: Seek immediate medical attention or contact emergency services if an overdose is suspected.

Q: Is it safe to buy medicines online?
A: Only purchase medicines from licensed and reputable pharmacies to avoid counterfeit products.
