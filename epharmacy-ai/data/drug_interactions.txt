Paracetamol + Alcohol:
Increased risk of liver toxicity, especially with chronic alcohol use.

Paracetamol + Warfarin:
Prolonged use may enhance anticoagulant effect and increase bleeding risk.

Ibuprofen + Aspirin:
Increased risk of gastrointestinal bleeding. Ibuprofen may reduce aspirinâ€™s cardioprotective effect if taken together.

Ibuprofen + Warfarin:
Significantly increased risk of bleeding. Use only under medical supervision.

Ibuprofen + Corticosteroids:
Increased risk of gastrointestinal ulceration and bleeding.

Aspirin + Warfarin:
High risk of bleeding. Avoid combination unless explicitly prescribed.

Aspirin + Methotrexate:
Reduced methotrexate clearance leading to toxicity.

Aspirin + Alcohol:
Increased risk of stomach bleeding.

Amoxicillin + Oral Contraceptives:
Rare reduction in contraceptive effectiveness. Advise backup contraception.

Amoxicillin + Allopurinol:
Increased risk of skin rash.

Metformin + Alcohol:
Increased risk of lactic acidosis. Avoid excessive alcohol intake.

Metformin + Cimetidine:
May increase metformin levels and risk of lactic acidosis.

Cetirizine + Alcohol:
Enhanced sedation and impaired coordination.

Cetirizine + CNS Depressants:
Increased drowsiness and reduced alertness.

Warfarin + Antibiotics:
Some antibiotics may increase bleeding risk by altering gut flora.

NSAIDs + Antihypertensives:
Reduced blood pressure control and increased kidney risk.

NSAIDs + Diuretics:
Reduced diuretic effect and increased risk of kidney injury.

Antidiabetic Drugs + Beta Blockers:
May mask symptoms of hypoglycemia.

ACE Inhibitors + Potassium Supplements:
Risk of hyperkalemia.

Statins + Grapefruit Juice:
Increased risk of muscle toxicity (varies by statin).

Benzodiazepines + Opioids:
Severe respiratory depression risk. Avoid combination.

SSRIs + NSAIDs:
Increased risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.

MAO Inhibitors + SSRIs:
Risk of serotonin syndrome. Contraindicated combination.
